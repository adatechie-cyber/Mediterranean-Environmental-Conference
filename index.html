About the Conference

The Mediterranean Environmental Conference brings together experts, researchers, and policymakers from Spain, Portugal, and North Africa to discuss critical environmental challenges in the region. Organized by BarcelonaReAct in collaboration with UOC, USAL, URJC, and UNESCO, this conference aims to foster interdisciplinary dialogue and develop actionable solutions.

Over three days, participants will engage in keynote presentations, panel discussions, and workshops focused on four main thematic areas: Environmental Impact Assessments, Biodiversity Action Plans, Flora and Fauna Management Plans, and Critical Habitat Assessments.

Conference Topics

Environmental Impact Assessments

Exploring methodologies for comprehensive EIAs in Mediterranean ecosystems, with case studies from participating regions.

Biodiversity Action Plans

Developing and implementing effective strategies for biodiversity conservation across national borders.

Flora and Fauna Management

Sustainable approaches to managing Mediterranean species and habitats in changing climate conditions.

Critical Habitat Assessments

Identifying and protecting critical habitats through advanced assessment techniques and policy frameworks.
